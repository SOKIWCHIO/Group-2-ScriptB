Introducing a groundbreaking advancement in hypothesis testing: 

Credal Two-Sample Testing 🧠📊 

This innovative framework allows for the comparison of credal sets—convex sets of probability measures that encapsulate both aleatoric and epistemic uncertainties. Unlike traditional two-sample tests that focus solely on precise distributions, this new approach accommodates the complexities of partial ignorance, thereby expanding the scope of hypotheses available for analysis.

Key Features: 

- Direct integration of epistemic uncertainty
- Enhanced reasoning capabilities for equality, inclusion, intersection, and mutual exclusivity
- Focus on finitely generated credal sets derived from independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) samples 
- Introduction of a permutation-based solution to improve robustness in hypothesis testing

This pioneering work represents a significant leap in nonparametric hypothesis testing methodologies. By effectively incorporating the modeller's epistemic beliefs into statistical analysis, it paves the way for more credible conclusions across various real-world applications.
