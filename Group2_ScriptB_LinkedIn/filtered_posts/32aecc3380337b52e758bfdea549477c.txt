⚠️ Your deep learning model might be confidently wrong — and you wouldn’t even know it.

In high-stakes fields like medicine and epidemiology, a wrong prediction can have serious consequences, which is why knowing how uncertain your model is matters just as much as the prediction itself.

In this episode, Alexandre Andorra talks with Mélodie Monod, François-Xavier Briol, and Yingzhen Li about the latest in Bayesian Deep Learning — and how a statistician’s mindset can make neural networks more reliable:

💡 Diffusion methods for uncertainty quantification
💡 The computational challenges of Bayesian neural networks
💡 How marginal likelihood can mislead model selection
💡 Spotting out-of-distribution examples early
💡 The exploration–exploitation trade-off in reinforcement learning
💡 Applying Bayesian methods to large language models
💡 Why generalised Bayesian inference may be more robust
