Excited to share our latest work published in IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems 
“Bayesian Neural Networks with Physics-Informed Priors with Application to Boundary Layer Velocity” — co-authored with Luca Menicali and David Richter — bridges machine learning and physical science by incorporating partial differential equations (PDEs) directly into the prior of a Bayesian neural network.

Why this matters:
Most physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) penalize PDE violations via the loss function.
We take a fully Bayesian approach: the physics becomes the prior, and uncertainty is naturally quantified through the posterior.
This method allows us to make physically consistent predictions with very limited data — even for complex systems like turbulent boundary layers.

Check out the paper here: https://lnkd.in/gqDb9wGs
