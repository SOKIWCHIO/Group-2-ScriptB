I am happy to share that my paper "Reusability of Bayesian Networks Case Studies: A Survey" has been published in Applied Intelligence! 

Bayesian Networks are widely used in fields like medicine, engineering, and environmental science, yet surprisingly, most academic papers do not provide enough details to allow others to reuse the models they present. 

In our study (Adarsa Sivaprasad, Ehud Reiter, Alberto J. Bugarín Diz), we analyzed 147 papers and found that only 18% included all the necessary information for the corresponding Bayesian Network reusability. 

This highlights a significant gap in research transparency and reproducibility—if we want AI and probabilistic models to have a real-world impact, we need to make them accessible and reusable.
