I’m pleased to share our latest work, now published in Knowledge-Based Systems:

"Most likely heteroscedastic Gaussian process via kernel smoothing"
🔗 https://lnkd.in/eQv2ZwxV

In this paper, we present a method for heteroscedastic Gaussian process regression that reduces computational cost by requiring only one GP model instead of two. By estimating noise levels through kernel smoothing, our approach lowers complexity from O(2N³) to O(N³ + N²), resulting in roughly 2× faster training in our experiments. We also observe improved or more stable performance in several test cases, including Bayesian optimisation tasks.

Many thanks to my co-authors Nasrulloh Loka, Hans Hallez, Keivan Shariatmadar PhD MSc BSc, and David Moens for their contributions and collaboration!
