Explainable AI (XAI) & Uncertainty Quantification (UQ):

What: Addressing the "black box" nature of complex AI models to build trust and understand their predictions, especially critical for high-stakes decisions.

Advanced Applications:

Interpretability Techniques: Using methods like SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations), LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations), and attention maps to understand why an AI model made a specific prediction (e.g., why it flagged this area as a fault).

Uncertainty-Aware Modeling: Developing AI models that explicitly quantify the uncertainty associated with their predictions (e.g., Bayesian Neural Networks, Deep Ensembles, Conformal Prediction). This is vital for risk assessment in exploration, drilling, and hazard mitigation.

Decision Support Systems: Integrating XAI and UQ outputs into user-friendly interfaces that provide geoscientists and engineers with transparent predictions and confidence levels, supporting informed decision-making.
