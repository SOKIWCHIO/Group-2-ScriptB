3D Occupancy Prediction has become a very active field in robotics and AI, but can we actually trust these predictions ?

We propose an adaptation of an efficient uncertainty estimation
technique to disentangle epistemic and aleatoric uncertainty for voxel-wise occupancy predictions. This allows us to dynamically calibrate model confidences, enhancing safety by handling adverse conditions (snow, sensor failures, etc.) and by providing uncertainty indicators for rare or unseen objects. Our approach outperforms standard baselines, offering more reliable and robust 3d occupancy predictions.
