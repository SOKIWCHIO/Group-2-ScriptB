Prediction Intervals can be used to quantify the uncertainty around predictions made by Machine Learning models. In other words they help us quantify the confidence we should have in predictions made by any ML model. Quantile Regression, traditional resampling (Bootstrap or Jacknife) and Bayesian approach can be used to obtain these intervals. However, these methods suffer from lack of theoretical guarantee, are computationally expensive, or are very dependent on the model/problem. 

MAPIE implements state-of-the-art resampling methods to provide well-calibrated prediction intervals with theoretical guarantee for any ML model trained using Sklearn package. #machinelearning #artificialintelligence #ml

With MAPIE, uncertainties are back in machine learning !
