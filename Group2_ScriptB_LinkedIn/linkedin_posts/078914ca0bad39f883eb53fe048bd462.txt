As we deploy machine learning models in drug discovery, it's essential to provide intuitive confidence estimates for the predictions. A new paper from my former colleagues Steven Kearnes and Patrick Riley, describes a method for calibrating the uncertainty of regression models.  The authors also provide a GitHub repository with code and example data.  Kudos (other) Pat and Steve.

Our ALOHA paper from 2012 puts everything in probability space from 0 to 1, which is essentially a smoothing of ordinal space (achieved by binning and spline fitting). Further advantage is then it’s very easy to combine scores by addition and you get nice Poisson Binomial distribution function behavior upon the combination of many terms.
