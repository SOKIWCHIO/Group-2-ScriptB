This study compares Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) to traditional machine learning models for classifying COVID-19 from chest X-rays. Key takeaways:

➡ BNNs help mitigate overfitting by sampling from parameter distributions and offer better-calibrated uncertainty estimates.
 ➡  Model ensembling—combining multiple top-performing models—boosts accuracy and confidence.
➡  Simpler ML methods (e.g., random forests, Gaussian processes) often match or outperform deep learning in binary classification settings.
 ➡  Highlights the importance of uncertainty quantification in high-stakes applications like medical diagnostics.
