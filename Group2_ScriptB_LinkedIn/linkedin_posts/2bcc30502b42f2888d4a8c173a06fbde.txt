If you want to go from ML to Deep Learning in Bayesian inference; here's your list of topics you must read

1. Bayes’ Theorem & Priors (the OG formula; understand this intuitively before anything else)
2. Maximum Likelihood vs Maximum A Posteriori (how priors work)
3. Bayesian Linear Regression (start simple; it’s linear regression with uncertainty)
4. Monte Carlo Methods (MCMC)
5. ELBO (Evidence Lower Bound; what everyone optimizes but few explain clearly)
6. Reparameterization Trick (used in VAEs too; core idea for differentiable sampling)
7. Bayesian Neural Networks (uncertainty-aware deep models; start with weight distributions)
8. Dropout as Bayesian Approximation (yes, your regular dropout kinda does Bayesian stuff)
