I am big believer in trying to understand the uncertainty of machine learning models before you roll them out. 

It just makes using the model in a decision process so much easier. All models are wrong sometimes and understanding uncertainty will let you value, control and mitigate those instances in advance.

"Conformal Prediction" is a technique making the rounds for estimating uncertainty intervals in black box models. I'm betting that its going to be very popular!

If you can stomach a bit of math, check out this paper giving an introduction to the topic. Highly recommended.

A Gentle Introduction to Conformal Prediction and Distribution-Free Uncertainty Quantification
