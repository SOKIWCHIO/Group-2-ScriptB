🎉Excited to share that our paper "Conformalized Credal Set Predictors" has been accepted at NeurIPS! If you're interested in uncertainty quantification and conformal prediction, check it out! (See Image 1)
📖 https://lnkd.in/dxGFhZ9g
✍️Alireza Javanmardi David Stutz Eyke Hüllermeier

A credal set is a set of probability distributions for a given instance, capturing both:
- Epistemic uncertainty: from the model's lack of knowledge.
- Aleatoric uncertainty: due to inherent randomness in the data (see Image 2).

💡 To construct valid credal sets with conformal prediction, we require first-order data 📊 - data where each instance is associated with a probability distribution over classes. Such data is increasingly available, e.g., via aggregated annotations from multiple annotators (see Image 3).

Whether the underlying model is a probabilistic classifier (first-order predictor) or a second-order predictor (e.g., BNN or evidential), we propose nonconformity functions that turn these models into valid credal set predictors 😎 (see Image 4).

📉 On real-world datasets like ChaosNLI and CIFAR10-H, we demonstrate that our credal sets are valid while comparing different nonconformity functions in terms of efficiency and their ability to disentangle aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty (see Image 5).
