Bayesian Neural Network is not just about uncertainty over weights. Not only weights, the type of activation functions, number of layers, their hidden dimension and other factors defining the architecture of network can also be treated as random variables. Yet the most famous variant captures only the uncertainty of weights to make inference easier.
For larger neural networks, you don't have to take the uncertainty of all weights into account, you can perform inference over a subset of weights and train the rest of them as deterministic parameters using usual optimizers.

In the video you can see trajectories of Hamiltonian Monte Carlo drawing samples from the posterior of a BNN for a classification problem. On the left you can see the neural network with current HMC position and on the right some of the posterior marginals.
