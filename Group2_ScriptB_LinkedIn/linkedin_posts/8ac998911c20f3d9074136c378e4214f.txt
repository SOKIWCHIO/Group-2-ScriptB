4 年

Performing probabilistic reasoning over the weights of Deep Learning models is analytically intractable. Furthermore, the vast size of the weight space limits us to using crude approximations when working with these models. 

In our most recent work “Expressive yet Tractable Bayesian Deep Learning via Subnetwork Inference”, we show how performing rich approximate inference over a carefully chosen subset of the weights works better than traditional Bayesian Deep Learning approximations. Our approach is competitive with the state of the art “Deep Ensembles“ despite being significantly cheaper computationally!

You can find a preprint of the paper at: https://lnkd.in/dD8Gzgu
