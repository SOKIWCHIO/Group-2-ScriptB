GOING BEYOND LLM: 
As we continue to witness the rapid evolution of AI, Large Language Models (LLMs) have become a cornerstone in the field. However, it's important to remember that LLMs are just the beginning, not the end goal. In the coming year, we can expect a shift towards multi-modal foundational models, expanding the capabilities of AI beyond just language understanding.

I've recently come across a paper that provides an insightful perspective on this evolution. The authors propose a computational framework called 'rational meaning construction' that goes beyond LLMs. Here are the key takeaways:

1. Integration of Models: The framework integrates LLMs with probabilistic models for rational inference. This approach maps natural language into a probabilistic language of thought, creating a unified interface for commonsense thinking from language.

2. Demonstration Across Domains: The framework is demonstrated across four cognitive science domains: probabilistic reasoning, logical and relational reasoning, visual and physical reasoning, and social reasoning about agents and their plans.

3. Context-Sensitive Translations: The authors show how LLMs can generate context-sensitive translations that capture linguistic meanings, while Bayesian inference supports robust commonsense reasoning.

4. Extension to Symbolic Modules: The authors extend the framework to include cognitively-motivated symbolic modules like physics simulators and planning algorithms.

5. Language as a Driver: The paper explores how language can drive the construction of world models.
