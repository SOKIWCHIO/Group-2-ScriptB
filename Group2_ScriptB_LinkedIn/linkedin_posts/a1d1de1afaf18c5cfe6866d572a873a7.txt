High-resolution maps of climate and ecosystem variables are essential for estimating carbon stocks, tracking ecosystem changes, and supporting climate policies. These maps, often created using remote sensing and machine learning, include uncertainty estimatesâ€”but these are not always assessed correctly.

A key issue is that spatial correlation in map errors is often ignored, leading to inaccurate uncertainty estimates when this uncertainty is aggregated at regional or national levels. This has real implications for applications like soil carbon MRV, where proper uncertainty assessment is crucial for carbon crediting and reporting.

Our note in International Journal of Applied Earth Observation and Geoinformation highlights this issue and discusses statistical methods to improve uncertainty quantification in environmental mapping.
