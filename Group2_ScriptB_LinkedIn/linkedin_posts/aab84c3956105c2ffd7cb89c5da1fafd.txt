The Water Resources Institute at UVM

7 个月  已编辑

Using AI and sensors, Vermont scientists have hacked the stream flow model to produce water quality forecasts. New research led by University of Vermont scientist, Dr. Andrew Schroth, and Utah State University scientist, Dr. John Kemper, shows how an already-existing computer system—the federal government’s National Water Model—can be modified, with AI and real-time data from sensors, to go beyond simply forecasting stream flow—to predicting water quality too. 

Read more about the first ever application of the National Water Model to predict water quality and its implications across the country.
