#Biological #neuralnetworks excel at solving complex problems and #predicting from noisy data. Similarly, #artificial #neuralnetworks (#ANNs) are powerful tools for pattern recognition but act as "point estimates," lacking the ability to capture uncertainty in #probabilistic #predictions. Modeling ANNs probabilistically via Bayesian inference poses challenges for traditional deterministic computing.

#Chaotic #light combined with #incoherent #photonic #processing enables high-speed probabilistic computation and uncertainty quantification.

Frank Brückerhoff-Plückelmann, Wolfram Pernice, and colleagues utilize a #photonic #probabilistic #architecture to perform image classification and uncertainty prediction through a #Bayesian #neuralnetworks. Their prototype integrates a physical entropy source with a computational architecture, achieving #ultrafast #probabilistic #computation via parallel sampling.

This work was done in collaboration with the University of Münster, Heidelberg University, University of Oxford, and University of Exeter.
