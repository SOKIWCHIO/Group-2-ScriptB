Announcing our latest publication in Transportation Research Part C: Emerging Technologies journal. Thanks to the amazing collaborators from Penn State University Transportation Engineering Lab - Dr. Ilgin Guler, Adway Das, and Dr. Sudeepta Mondal for their contributions! 

Traffic prediction models often struggle to adapt to out-of-distribution datasets due to limited generalizability. Our recent paper introduces a Bayesian recurrent neural network framework for uncertainty quantification in traffic prediction with enhanced generalizability by introducing spectral normalization to its hidden layers. By controlling complexity and mitigating overfitting, our method improves performance even with limited training data. Additionally, prediction intervals empower traffic management systems to make real-time informed decisions and address congestion effectively. Our innovation presents a significant advancement in traffic prediction, offering practical solutions for real-world mobility challenges.
